    Final straw for plastic pollution?

    9 in 10 worldwide would support a ban on plastic straws in bars and restaurants to help protect environment

    Norval Scott
    Apr 25, 2018 · 2 min read

    Since Sir David Attenborough highlighted the problem of plastic waste for marine life in the BBC’s Blue Planet documentaries, restaurant and bar chains across the world have been ditching the humble plastic straw.

    In the UK, the London Evening Standard has loudly campaigned for an outright ban, and the government may act this year to outlaw the single-use drinking vessel. But at Streetbees, we wondered — what do the people think?

    We carried out a straw poll of over 1,700 bees worldwide — and discovered overwhelming public support for a ban, spanning every nationality and age group.

    Across the US, UK, South Africa and India, we found that 9 in 10 would support a ban on plastic straws in bars and restaurants to help protect the environment, while 4 in 5 said that plastic straws are ‘very harmful’ to the environment.

    Even more surprisingly, more than 1 in 2 (53%) said plastic waste is the biggest threat to the world’s oceans, much more than toxic waste/sewage (17%) or global warming (12%).

    There wasn’t much in the way of opposition — indeed, this is one of the most one-sided surveys Streetbees has ever carried out. That said, 1 in 4 say they still use a straw at least once a week or more — which is a lot of plastic waste worldwide.

    —

    The figures in the article were taken in April 2018 from 1,702 people across the UK, US, India and South Africa. All of the data was collected by mobile and web surveys, and is accurate to within 3 percentage points 19 times out of 20.
